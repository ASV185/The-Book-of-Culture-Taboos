Spain Cultural Taboos & Practices

Punctuality is Not Important

People are not expected to arrive on time for everything. So do not get offended if someone is late in Spain.

Expected Meals are Served at Later Times

In Spain, meals are served at later times. Most restaurants open later for all three meals. Lunch is served at 2 or 3 in the afternoon, and dinner at 9 at night.

Greeting Others

Women are expected the greet by giving two kisses, one on each cheek. Men can greet each other by hugging or shaking hands.

Impolite to Waste Food

If you were to order food or serve food by someone, it is expected for you to eat all of it. It is better not to ask for food or decline offer of food from someone if you won't eat it.

Appropriate Attire

You need to wear appropriate attire if you were to visit Spain. For example, if you were to visit a church, you should wear church attire.

Do Not Ignore Regional Differences

There a regional differences and people are expected to follow them. For example, Barcelona is located in the province of Catalonia, and it has its own unique culture and traditions.

Flowers are Not Given as a Gift to Just Anyone

In Spain, you have to be careful if you give flowers to someone. Red roses are for romantic occasions, and white lilies and chrysanthemums are given at funerals.
