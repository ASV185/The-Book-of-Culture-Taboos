Nigeria Cultural Taboos & Practices

Men's Herds

In Nigeria, it is rude to ask how big a man's herd is especially in Nigeria's Yoruba society.

Greetings Should NOT Be First

It is considered a taboo to introduce yourself before asking about a person's well being first.

Chopping Fire Wood at Night

The Nigerian people have a superstitution that if you chop fire wood at night, you can turn into a statue.

Do Not Use Left Hand Frequently

In the Urhobo tribe, it is considered unclean and an insult to just use your left hand to do something especially to eat. The actions you take should be completed by using your right hand or both hands.

Religion

In Nigeria, it is rude for visitors or tourists to make generalizations of religion. Nigeria is a very diverse country that includes various traditions and tribes.

Traditional Nigerian Clothes

It is frown upon for tourists or non-natives in Nigeria to wear traditional clothing. It can be seen as an insult and you should refrain from wearing them if you are not a native of Nigeria.

Suicide

Even though it has always been a taboo, it is now a crime to commit suicide in Nigeria. When someone commits suicide the whole family is punished, and must pay extensive fines.

Yams and Sweet Potatoes

In August, it is forbidden to eat yams or sweet potatoes. This taboo mainly applies to the Igbo tribe in Nigeria.

You Should Not Kill Monkeys or Snakes

In Idemilli, it is a taboo and a crime to kill monkeys or snakes.
