India Cultural Taboos

To Touch Someone on the Top of Their Head is Rude and Insensitive

In India, the top of someone's head is considered the most important part of the body. Therefore, it is rude and insensitive for someone to touch it especially babies, children, elderly, religious leaders, or statues of deities.

Never Sit Higher Than An Elder

You should never sit higher than an elder. If they are sitting down, you should sit down too.

Do Not Pass Objects with Only the Left Hand

The left hand is usually used for cleaning, and never for passing an object. You should use the right hand or both hands to pass an object.

Do Not Wear Shoes in Someone's House

You should remove your shoes before entering someone's home, and other places such as Jain or Hindu Temples.

Some Gifts are Inappropriate Depending on One's Religious Affiliation

You should be careful to give gifts. Some gifts are not appropriate depending on someone's religious affiliation. For example, giving gifts made out of leather may offend someone that is Hindu. If someone is Muslim, giving a gift related to pigs may be offensive.

Do Not Step Over a Person Sitting or Laying on the Floor

In India, feet and shoes are considered dirty. You should never step over a person sitting or Laying on the floor because it is offensive.

Avoid Shaking Hands Unless the Person Greets You First

In India, men and women do not usually touch so you need to be careful. You should wait for person to greet you first.
